we have a distribution problem, and it's a habit, not a skill gap.
when we release something, we treat "published" as "finished." we post it once, get a bit of recognition, feel good, and move to the next thing. but publishing once isn't distributing. by the time we're sick of repeating ourselves, most of our audience hasn't heard about it even once.
new rule: a release isn't done when it goes live. it's done when we've pushed it for weeks — repeated it, reshared it, shown it to people who missed it the first time. we optimize for impact, not for the feeling of completion.
if something works even a little, that's the signal to push harder, not to move on.
